The Problem

Global platforms tax local economies they don’t understand.

In emerging tourism regions like the Caucasus, Central Asia, and the Caspian, hosts are forced into platforms that take a cut of every booking — while travelers can’t find them in the first place.

15%

Hosts

Average commission charged by global platforms — the difference between a profit and a hobby.

For a host with 30 bookings/year at ₽4,000/night, that’s ~₽58,000 disappearing into a platform that has never set foot in their village.

5+

Travelers

Channels a traveler must check to plan one trip — Avito, Суточно.ру, Telegram, Instagram, WhatsApp.

No single trusted source. The trip plan ends with a phone call to a friend who knows someone who knows someone.

02 / 12 YURT — CONCEPT 2026

Why Now

A regional tourism market reshaped in three years.

2022 → 2025

Domestic tourism reset

Russian outbound travel collapsed; domestic and CIS tourism grew 40 %+ year-on-year. Caucasus regions reported record overnight stays.

2024 → today

Booking left the building

Booking.com and Airbnb withdrew or restricted service in Russia. Hosts scattered across Avito, Суточно.ру, and Telegram — none of which were designed for guesthouse hosts.

Now

An open category

No single trusted brand serves regional Russia and the wider Caucasus corridor. Smartphone penetration is at 87 %; mobile payments are normalized. The window is open — briefly.

The Solution

A trusted booking platform built for the region, not adapted to it.

Yurt brings hosts and travelers into one space — with pricing, tools, and trust built around how small local economies actually work.

01.

One Place, Not Five

Stays, routes, and local services consolidated into a single trusted experience — replacing the chain of phone calls and Telegram links.

02.

Hosts Pay a Flat Fee

$89/year flat replaces 12–18 % per-booking commission. The 30th booking costs the same as the first: zero.

03.

Guided By Locals

Recommendations come from people who actually live there. The host’s voice is the listing — not a stock description.

Go-to-Market

Win one valley, then the next.

A geographic-and-network strategy built around founder-host distribution — not a paid funnel.

100 hosts · 2 regions

Phase 02 · Months 6–18

Expansion across the North Caucasus

Host-led referral program. Local-language Telegram and Instagram, run by hosts for hosts. Tour-operator and guide partnerships compound the supply side.

1,500 hosts · 6 regions

Phase 03 · Months 18–36

Caucasus corridor & Central Asia

Cross-border to Georgia, Armenia, Kazakhstan. Same playbook, replicated by regional ambassadors. Yurt becomes the trusted marketplace for the corridor.

12K hosts · ARR $1M+

Moat

The defensibility is not the pricing — it’s the founder-host distribution and the trust loop. Avito can copy a flat fee. Avito cannot copy a Balkar founder who hosts in Terskol and knows by name the eleven women running guesthouses on her road. That asymmetry compounds with every host we onboard.
